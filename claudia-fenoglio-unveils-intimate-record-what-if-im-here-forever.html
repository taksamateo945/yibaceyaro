Claudia Fenoglio Unveils Intimate Record 'What if I'm Here Forever'

 Posted on August 29, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  451 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Leeds-native Claudia Fenoglio has unveiled her latest musical offering, “What if I’m Here Forever”, on September 15th. This EP is a testament to her artistic growth, reaffirming her signature sound while showcasing a striking evolution in her musical prowess.

Fenoglio’s musical odyssey commenced with a serendipitous visit to Nashville, where the acoustic music captured her soul and kindled her passion for the guitar. She made waves with her debut single ‘Waste,’ amassing an impressive 30,000 Spotify streams within just one month. Her on-stage presence has been notable at Live at Leeds, Long Division Festival, and Wimin Festival, sharing the spotlight alongside artists such as Abbie Ozard, Rianne Downey, and Maria BC!

Building on the success of previously released singles like “19 Again” and “Like it Never Was,” she channels influences from acclaimed artists such as Taylor Swift, Julia Jacklin, and Phoebe Bridgers, carving her unique path in the musical landscape.

The compact 14-minute EP, “What if I’m Here Forever,” opens with previously released tracks like “Alaska,” “Forever,” and “Girlfriends On TV,” each offering a distinct musical experience that encapsulates Fenoglio’s ethereal vocals and poignant storytelling. These tracks are born from intimate moments, crafted as she sat with her acoustic guitar, shaping melodies and visions that would become lyrical stories touching on personal experiences, past relationships, nostalgia, queer love, and beloved TV show characters.

Collaborating with producer Tim Malkin, Fenoglio delivers four songs that vividly portray the life of a 25-year-old artist navigating life’s complexities. Tracks like “Alaska” and “Forever” display her knack for playful drums, chorus-soaked guitars, and heavenly backing vocals, immersing listeners in a world of raw emotion. Meanwhile, “Girlfriends On TV” delves into indie-rock influences, boasting hard-hitting drums, swirling guitar melodies, and irresistibly catchy hooks.

The EP’s focal track, “Stay With You,” stands as the crowning jewel, captivating audiences from the first note. It embodies Fenoglio’s essence as an artist, weaving a tapestry of acoustic chords, vibrant counter melodies, and a rich chorus that envelopes listeners in its depth. Her warm vocals take centre stage, supported by layers of backing vocals, creating a mesmerizing auditory experience culminating in a cinematic finale.

Overall, “What if I’m Here Forever” offers an immersive and emotionally resonant journey through life’s complexities. Fenoglio’s music captures the bittersweet intricacies of modern life, creating intimate soundscapes that resonate with listeners. Her unique blend of “sad-girl indie” and “karaoke pop bangers” promises to captivate audiences with its lasting appeal. This musical gem is not to be missed; it’s a transformative experience poised to leave a long-lasting indie music legacy.
