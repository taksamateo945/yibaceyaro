The Criteria Used for Ranking

When determining the list of the greatest footballers ever, a comprehensive set of criteria was employed. These factors ensured that iconic soccer stars who had left an indelible mark on the sport were properly recognized and celebrated.

  • Importance to the Team: One of the key considerations was the player’s significance to their team. This encompassed their leadership qualities, influence on the game, and ability to inspire and motivate their teammates.
  • Success in Trophies: Winning club and international trophies played a pivotal role in ranking these legendary footballers. Their contributions to their team’s achievements, including league titles, domestic cups, and prestigious international tournaments, were carefully evaluated.
  • Individual Awards and Accolades: Another crucial factor was the player’s individual success and recognition. Achievements such as Ballon d’Or awards, MVP titles, and Golden Boots were taken into account, highlighting their exceptional skills and abilities.

This holistic approach to ranking ensured that the selected footballers had made significant contributions and achieved great success throughout their careers. Now, let’s take a closer look at each of these remarkable athletes and discover why they are considered the greatest footballers ever.

Lionel Messi – The GOAT

Lionel Messi is widely regarded as one of the greatest soccer athletes and all-time best footballers in history. With an impressive 1047 appearances and a plethora of accolades, including multiple Ballon d’Or awards, Messi has consistently demonstrated exceptional skill, finesse, and goal-scoring ability on the field.

Throughout his career, Messi has mesmerized fans with his incredible dribbling, close ball control, and precise passing. His ability to create scoring opportunities for himself and his teammates is unparalleled. Messi’s agility, speed, and vision allow him to effortlessly navigate through defenses, leaving defenders in awe.

What truly sets Messi apart is his incredible consistency and longevity at the highest level of the game. He has consistently delivered outstanding performances year after year, breaking numerous records along the way. Messi’s impact on the game is undeniable, inspiring and influencing future generations of players.

“Messi is a once-in-a-lifetime talent. His skill, dedication, and love for the game are unparalleled. It’s a privilege to witness his brilliance on the field.” – Diego Maradona

Revered by fans and respected by his peers, Messi’s contributions to the sport have earned him a special place in football history. He has led FC Barcelona to numerous domestic and international titles, including four UEFA Champions League triumphs. Messi’s achievements have also extended to the international stage, where he has represented Argentina with distinction.

Throughout his career, Messi has won the hearts of soccer fans around the world with his humble demeanor and sportsmanship. His passion for the game and his relentless pursuit of excellence have made him an idol for aspiring footballers globally.

The impact of Lionel Messi on the game is immeasurable. He consistently raises the bar for what is possible on the football field, showcasing the epitome of skill, creativity, and athleticism. Messi’s legacy as one of the greatest footballers of all time is secure, and his influence will continue to shape the future of the beautiful game.

Diego Maradona – The Hand of God

Diego Maradona, with 428 appearances, is renowned for his incredible skill and flair on the pitch. His infamous “Hand of God” goal and his mesmerizing dribbling abilities showcased his unique talent. Maradona’s impact on the game and his status as an Argentine footballing legend make him one of the greatest footballers to have ever played.

Diego Maradona was an exceptional talent who captivated audiences with his sublime footballing abilities. His skill and creativity on the field were unparalleled, allowing him to excel in every aspect of the game. Maradona’s career was filled with iconic moments, but none more so than his infamous “Hand of God” goal in the 1986 World Cup quarter-final against England. This audacious act demonstrated his resourcefulness and the extent to which he was willing to push the boundaries to secure victory.

“The Hand of God” – as Maradona’s goal came to be known – is a testament to his cunning and ability to seize opportunities in the most unexpected ways. It showcased his quick thinking, audacity, and natural instinct to exploit any advantage, no matter how controversial. This goal remains one of the most memorable and debated moments in football history, solidifying Maradona’s place in the annals of the sport.

However, Maradona’s talent extended far beyond a single controversial incident. His mesmerizing dribbling skills, lightning-quick agility, and superb ball control made him virtually untouchable on the field. Maradona was capable of effortlessly gliding past multiple defenders, leaving them in his wake as if they were mere spectators. His ability to change the course of a game single-handedly is a testament to his brilliance as a player.

Maradona’s impact on the game extended beyond his individual achievements. As the captain of the Argentine national team, he led his country to victory in the 1986 World Cup, showcasing both his leadership qualities and exceptional skills. His performances during this tournament, especially against England and Belgium, solidified his status as one of the greatest footballers of all time.

In addition to his international success, Maradona achieved great heights at the club level. His time at Napoli in the late 1980s and early 1990s brought unprecedented success to the club, including two Serie A titles and a UEFA Cup. Maradona’s influence not only transformed Napoli into a force to be reckoned with but also elevated the profile of Italian football worldwide.

Maradona’s Style of Play

Maradona possessed a unique combination of technical skill, agility, and vision on the field. His exceptional balance and low center of gravity allowed him to maintain close control of the ball even in tight spaces, making it nearly impossible for defenders to dispossess him. Maradona’s dribbling was characterized by his trademark “slalom” technique, effortlessly weaving past opponents with intricate footwork.

Furthermore, Maradona possessed incredible vision and the ability to read the game with unmatched accuracy. His pinpoint passes and intricate through balls made him a deadly playmaker, creating numerous scoring opportunities for his teammates.

Maradona’s Legacy

Diego Maradona’s impact on the game transcends his on-field achievements. He became an icon not only in Argentina but across the footballing world. His passion for the game, combined with his skill and flamboyant playing style, captured the hearts of millions of fans worldwide.

Maradona’s tragic passing in 2020 further cemented his status as a footballing legend. The outpouring of grief and tributes from players, fans, and clubs around the world served as a testament to the profound impact he had on the sport. Maradona’s legacy will forever endure, and his name will forever be associated with greatness in the world of football.

Zinedine Zidane – The French Maestro

Zinedine Zidane is widely regarded as one of the greatest footballers ever to grace the pitch. With 797 appearances under his belt, the French maestro mesmerized the footballing world with his exceptional technique, elegance, and playmaking abilities.

Known for his effortless control of the game, Zidane had the unique ability to dictate the tempo and rhythm of a match. His impeccable ball control, precise passing, and incredible vision allowed him to create scoring opportunities for his teammates and deliver magical moments on the field.

Throughout his career, Zidane showcased his talent on both domestic and international stages. He played a crucial role in leading the French national team to victory in the 1998 FIFA World Cup and the UEFA Euro 2000, leaving an indelible mark on French football history.

“Zidane… spectacular goal!” – Martin Tyler

Zidane’s impact as both a player and a coach further solidifies his status as one of the greatest footballers ever. After retiring as a player, he went on to achieve tremendous success as the manager of Real Madrid, guiding the team to multiple UEFA Champions League titles.

With his graceful style, extraordinary skillset, and undeniable impact, Zinedine Zidane’s name will forever be etched in the annals of football history.

Ronaldo, with 553 appearances, is widely regarded as one of the greatest soccer athletes and best footballers of all time. His ability to burst past defenders with lightning speed and navigate through tight spaces made him virtually unstoppable on the field. His ability to perform under immense pressure and deliver when it mattered most is a testament to his greatness.

Moreover, Ronaldo’s technical mastery was matched by his mental resilience. Despite facing setbacks in the form of injuries throughout his career, he made remarkable comebacks time and again, showcasing his determination and love for the game. His unwavering spirit and unwavering commitment to excellence serve as an inspiration to aspiring footballers.
