Career

The media personality commenced her professional journey in Miami, where she took on the role of executive producer at 790 AM The Ticket.

Subsequently, she became a co-host on the Zaslow and Joy Show, the station’s highly popular morning-drive sports radio program.

Her tenure with the network spanned three years.

Afterward, she became a part of CBSSports.com, taking on the roles of host for shows like Fantasy Football Today and Thursday Night Live.

In March 2016, she transitioned to FOX Sports, where she continues to work to this day, having taken on multiple positions within the network, including standing in for Kristine Leahy on The Herd with Colin Cowherd.

She was announced as the moderator for the sports debate show “Skip and Shannon: Undisputed” on August 15, 2016.

In this role, she collaborated closely with commentator Skip Bayless and sports analyst Shannon Sharpe.

In 2018, Fox revealed that Taylor would transition from her position on Undisputed to join The Herd with Colin Cowherd, a show broadcast on both Fox Sports 1 and Fox Sports Radio.

She officially took on her new role on June 18th, 2018.

The announcement came on September 9, 2021, that she would take on the role of host for the Saturday program, “The Joy Taylor Show,” on Fox Sports Radio.

In addition to her role as a host, she manages the Maybe I’m Crazy podcast where she dissects stories from the world of NFL, NBA, and various other sports.

Additionally, she leads The Hang on Facebook Live.

Who Is Filling In For Joy Taylor?

In March of 2022, Taylor’s absence from The Herd With Colin Cowherd was conspicuous to fans.

During that time, she decided to take a well-deserved break from her professional duties and embarked on a trip to Paris, France.

In her absence, the role of hosting the show was capably assumed by television host and Fox Sports reporter, Alex Curry.

Who Is Joy Taylor’s Husband?

Joy Taylor was married to Richard Giannotti from 2016 to 2017.

They initially crossed paths at a conference in 2014 and later embarked on a romantic relationship before getting married.

On the 13th of February 2016, Giannotti, who held the position of President at Athlete Asset Management, tied the knot with her at the opulent Biltmore luxury hotel in Miami.

Their union, however, proved to be brief, and the specific cause for their separation remained undisclosed to the public.

In 2017, the public figure made a shocking revelation about being a survivor of domestic abuse during an episode of Undisputed.

However, she chose not to disclose the identity of her abuser.

In September of 2018, it came to light that she had become engaged to Earl Watson, a former NBA point guard and head coach.

However, the two decided to end their engagement in 2019.

Subsequently, she has chosen to maintain a private stance on her romantic life, keeping it out of the public eye.
